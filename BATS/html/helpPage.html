Creating & Accesing your Account

Register User

To start using BATS, you will need to create an account.

  • From the BATS main page, click Register.
  • Create your username and email.
  • Next, you will need to create a password for your account. Type in your password twice and click Register to continue.

Log-in to User Account

To log in to an existing BATS user account

  • From the BATS main page, click Login.
  • Enter your Username and Password.
  • Click login to continue.

Creating & Viewing a Study

Creating a New Study

From the User Home Page, click the “New Study” button on the upper-right hand corner.

Complete the form with the information from your study.

  • Study Name: Complete study name
  • Short Study Name:
  • Abstract: Description of the Study (No longer than 500 characters)
  • Institutions Involved: Any institutions involved with the development of the study
  • Study Contact: Name and/or Email of any individual involved with the development of the study
  • Study Notes: Any pertinent information to the study
  • IRB Approved: The Institutional Review Board is group that has been formally designated to review and monitor biomedical research involving human subjects. In accordance with FDA regulations, an IRB has the authority to approve, require modifications in (to secure approval), or disapprove research. This group review serves an important role in the protection of the rights and welfare of human research subjects.

    Please check if your study has been approved by the IRB. This field can be edited later.

  • Visbility: Are other researchers involved in this study able to view its data?
  • Click "Create Study" to continue.

Viewing a Study

Once you have created a new study, you will be able to see your study in the User Home Page Studies table

To view your study, click over the study row in the studies table

This will take you to the study page.

Uploading & Viewing data

Uploading data

To upload new data, you will first need to create a new study.

Option 1: From User Home Page

  • From the User Home Page, click "Add new data" in the upper-right hand of the page.
  • This will take you to the uploading page
  • File: Upload a file from your computer
  • Name for data: Any identification for the data uploaded
  • Type of data:
    • Neural Data
    • Speech Data
    • Log Data
  • Then select the study to which you want to add the new uploaded data.
  • Click "Upload" to continue.

Option 2: From Study Page

  • From the page of a specific Study, click "Add new data" in the upper-left hand of the page.
  • File: Upload a file from your computer
  • Name for data: Any identification for the data uploaded
  • Type of data:
    • Neural Data
    • Speech Data
    • Log Data
  • Click "Upload" to continue.
Viewing Uploaded Data

View all uploaded data

  • From any page in our BATS application, click "View Data" from on the upper nav-bar.
  • A page with a table with all your uploaded data will appear.

View uploaded data for a specific study

  • On the page for a specific study you will be able to see a table titled "Data"
  • In this table you will be able to see all the data uploaded for this study

Graphing Data

To graph a .csv file containing data, first go to the Study Page where that data was uploaded.

  • From the Study Page page, click Graphing Toolkit under the Analysis Tools accordion.
  • Click the Graph CSV data row
  • Once inside the Data page, Select Data to graph, then press Next.
  • You should now be able to see the written data on the left side of your screen with its accompaning graph on the right.

Downloading data and Graph

The BATS systems provides the option to download your generated data and graphs

  • Once you have graphed a specific data, clic:
  • "Download Result file" to get the generated data file
  • "Download Graph" to get the generated graph .png

Analysing Data

To analyze a specific set of data, first go to the Study Page where that data was uploaded.

  • From the Study Page page, under the Analysis Tools accordion you will be able to find different types of Analysis Toolkits.

    Please take a moment to look over all the Analysis tools available

  • Click on the Analysis tool of your choice
  • Once inside the Data page, Select Data you wish to anaze, then press Next.
  • You should now be able to see the resulting data on the left side of your screen with its accompaning graph on the right.
